The Affiliate Revenue Formula

Three numbers compound into real income. Get them right and the rest follows.

STEP · 01

Traffic

Organic search visitors to your niche content pages. Quality matters more than quantity — 10,000 "best X for Y" search visitors convert 5× better than 50,000 social media clicks.

STEP · 02

CTR × Conversion

The click-through rate on your affiliate links and the conversion rate once visitors reach the merchant product page. These are your two biggest levers — improve CTR from 3% to 6% and you double revenue without more traffic.

STEP · 03

Commission × LTV

Average commission per sale, multiplied by any recurring revenue or lifetime value factor. Amazon Associates pays 1–10%. SaaS affiliate programs often pay $50–$200+ per referral, sometimes with recurring revenue.

FAQ

The questions every affiliate asks — answered with real numbers.

How much can you make with affiliate marketing?+

Beginner affiliate marketers typically make $0–$1,000/month in year 1. Intermediate marketers with 50K+ monthly visits make $1,000–$20,000/month. Top 10% affiliate sites generate $50,000+/month. Niche, monetization strategy, and SEO authority determine where you land. A single strong 10,000-visitor site in finance can make $20,000/month — a general lifestyle site with 100K visits might only make $2,000.

What is a good affiliate conversion rate?+

Average affiliate conversion rates are 0.5–3% depending on the niche and traffic quality. Finance and comparison pages typically convert at 2–5%. General content review pages convert at 0.8–2%. Product-focused buying-intent pages convert highest at 3–8%. A 1% conversion on 10,000 visits at $50 average commission = $5,000/month.

What is a good CTR for affiliate links?+

A good click-through rate (CTR) on affiliate links is 3–10%. Product comparison tables and well-placed CTA buttons at the top of high-intent pages can reach 15–25% CTR. Lower-ranked links in long-form content may only get 1–3% CTR. Average across a site is typically 2–5%.

How much traffic do I need to make $1,000/month from affiliate?+

Assuming a 5% CTR and 2% conversion rate and $75 average commission: $1,000/month requires approximately 13,500 visits/month (less than 500/day). In a high-commission niche like finance or hosting ($100+ per conversion), you could hit $1,000/month with only 3,000–5,000 visits/month. In low-commission niches like general beauty, you might need 50,000+ visits.

Which niches pay the highest affiliate commissions?+

Highest-paying affiliate niches: (1) Web hosting / SaaS — $50–$300+ recurring; (2) Finance / investing / credit cards — $50–$500; (3) Online courses / education — $50–$200 recurring; (4) CBD / supplements — $25–$100; (5) Luxury fashion / travel — $50–$200. Best strategy: a content site that ranks for product keywords and drives high-intent buyers.

How fast can a new affiliate site start making money?+

Realistic timeline for a new niche site: Months 1–3 = $0 (build phase, SEO indexing); Months 4–8 = first $100–$1,000; Months 9–18 = $1,000–$5,000; Year 2+ = strong sites hit $5,000–$50,000. The compounding nature of SEO means most of your income arrives 12+ months in — but it compounds for years once rankings lock in.
