How Email Performance Works

The three KPIs that drive email revenue.

STEP · 01

Open Rate = Opens ÷ Sent

Open rate measures how well your subject line and sender reputation perform. Average: 18% B2B, 14% B2C newsletters. Below 10% needs list hygiene or IP warmup.

STEP · 02

CTR = Clicks ÷ Sent

CTR measures how well your email body and CTA resonate. Average: 2.3% across industries. A strong email should hit 3–5% CTR; anything above 8% is elite content.

STEP · 03

CTOR = Clicks ÷ Opens

CTOR isolates email body content quality from subject-line quality. Average: 10–18%. A high CTOR means once subscribers open your email, your content converts — a clean signal that your subject line is the bottleneck.

FAQ

Top questions about email KPIs and campaign performance.

What is a good email open rate in 2026?+

A good email open rate in 2026 averages 18–25% for B2B and 14–22% for B2C newsletters. Welcome emails and transactional emails often hit 30–50%. Anything below 10% typically means list hygiene problems (bought list, missing double opt-in) or poor sender reputation (low IP warmup, high spam complaints).

What is click-to-open rate (CTOR) and why is it important?+

CTOR = (Clicks / Opens) × 100. Industry average is 10–18%. CTOR is more useful than raw CTR because it isolates subject-line performance from content performance. If CTOR is high but CTR is low, you have a subject-line problem. If CTOR is low, your body copy and CTA need work.

How do I calculate email ROI?+

Email ROI = ((Revenue − Cost) / Cost) × 100. If the only cost is your ESP and design/copywriting time, a $500 campaign generating $4,000 in revenue returns 700% ROI. Average email marketing ROI in 2026 is $42 return per $1 spent — the highest of any digital channel, which is why email still dominates DTC and SaaS onboarding budgets.

What is the difference between opens and unique opens?+

Opens counts every time an email is opened (including re-opens). Unique Opens counts each subscriber only once. A ratio of 1.3× (opens = 130% of unique opens) is healthy; above 1.6× suggests high-quality content being revisited — a signal you should double down on that content format.

How can I improve my email CTR?+

Six proven levers for higher email CTR: (1) Single, clear CTA above the fold; (2) Bullet-list scannable content; (3) Personalized dynamic content using first-name and segment data; (4) Time-of-day sending based on subscriber timezone; (5) Two-step subject-line preheader testing (preheaders lift open rate 5–15%); (6) List hygiene — remove subscribers who haven't opened in 6+ months.

What industry has the highest email conversion rate?+

Highest email conversion rates in 2026: Non-profit fundraising (3.4%); B2B SaaS (2.9%); E-commerce apparel (2.5%); Travel & hospitality (2.2%); Beauty & personal care (2.0%); Publishing & media (1.4%). Average across industries sits at 1.8% — use our calculator to see how your campaigns stack up.

How do you calculate cost per acquisition (CPA) from an email campaign?+

Email CPA = Total Cost of Campaign / Number of Conversions. Total cost includes ESP subscription, copywriting, design, list rental, and promotion spend. CPA of $15–$30 is typical for DTC; B2B SaaS CPA from email nurture is $150–$350. Compare CPA against your AOV and LTV — if CPA < 30% of LTV, the campaign is a keeper.
